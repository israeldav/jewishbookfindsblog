This is not an extremely interesting find, but still cool. It's a pair of Jewish almanacs in German from 1935 and 1936. There seems to be a range of essays on different aspects and topics of Judaism and Zionism. One page that was particularly interesting was the list of global Jewish occupancy in the 1936 pamphlet (last image below). The almanacs were published by Schocken Verlag - a publishing company founded and owned by Salman (Zalman) Schocken. Salman eventually moved to Palestine and purchased the Ha'aretz newspaper.
